Here are some tips for writing paragraphs: Structure A paragraph typically has three parts: an introduction, a body, and a conclusion. Introduction: The first part of the paragraph, which should include a topic sentence and any background information or transitions. Body: The middle part of the paragraph, which should discuss the main idea using facts, examples, and other information. Conclusion: The final part of the paragraph, which should summarize the main idea and the connections between the information in the body. Length Paragraphs should be between three and five sentences long, but can be longer if necessary. Very long paragraphs can be divided into two separate paragraphs. Ideas Each paragraph should focus on one idea. If you want to introduce a new idea, start a new paragraph. Transition words Use transition words or phrases between sentences and paragraphs to help wrap up ideas. Consistency Be consistent with point of view, verb tense, and number. Parallel structure Create parallel structure by using the same grammatical structure and parts of speech in multiple phrases or sentences. Repeat key words Repeat key words or phrases, especially when defining or identifying an important idea. Different types of paragraphs can be used to achieve different purposes, such as: Descriptive: Describe a person, place, object, or event in detail. Narrative: Tell a story or recount an event. Expository: Explain or inform about a topic. Persuasive: Convince the reader of a point of view. Compare and contrast: Highlight similarities and differences between two or more things. Cause and effect: Explain reasons and outcomes.
